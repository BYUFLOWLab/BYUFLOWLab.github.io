Useful Aircraft (and Rocket) Design Tools

Airfoil Analysis

  • XFOIL: viscous or inviscid aerodynamic performance of airfoils. Panel method with integral boundary layer formulation.
  • CloudFoil: runs SU2 for airfoils, as a RANS solver, in the cloud.

Wing/Aircraft Aerodynamics and Stability Derivatives

  • XFLR5: aerodynamics and stability derivatives of low Reynolds number aircraft. Uses XFOIL with a a vortex lattice method or a 3D panel method.
  • AVL: similar program based on an extended vortex lattice method. Less user-friendly, but a longer history of usage.
  • Tornado: a vortex lattice method in Matlab.

Geometry

  • OpenVSP: a parametric geometry modeling tool for aircraft. Includes some analysis capabilities as well through a vortex lattice method.
  • MachUp: geometry modeling tool with built-in nonlinear lifting line algorithm.

Propellers/Motors

  • DriveCalc: model RC airplane electric power system. Less features than MotoCalc but free and works on all operating systems.
  • MotoCalc: easy to use tool for modeling RC airplane electric power systems (only free for 30 days and only on Windows)
  • QPROP: propeller/motor analysis and design

Aircraft Design and Optimization

  • SUAVE: Python-based open-source conceptual aircraft design tool
  • CEASIOM

Flight Simulators

  • Gazebo: this is what we use to perform software-in-the-loop simulations using the exact same autopilot software that will be on your plane. Gazebo is setup on a computer in the FB.
  • FlightGear: connect RC transmitter and virtually flight test your aircraft by inputting mass properties and aerodynamic/control coefficients. It is also possible to simulate your autopilot, though this requires a fair amount of additional effort.
  • CRRCSim: similar.
