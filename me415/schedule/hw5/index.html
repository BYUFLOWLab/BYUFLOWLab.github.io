HW 5: Propulsion

due 10/25/2017 before midnight via Learning Suite 50 possible points

  1. Work on building your aircraft. The amount you accomplish this week is up to you (of course doing less now means more work later). A good goal would be to get it ready for RC flight. However far you get, for the purpose of this assignment you just need to:
    • Provide a detailed description of progress made, lessons learned, and any design changes.
    • Provide a few photos of the plane/process.

    Where do I get the electronic components?

    • The autopilot we provide, but they are still being assembled. Fortunately, you don’t need them to get started with RC testing. In the meantime we will provide you with a pod that is roughly the same size and weight. One thing to keep in mind is that one autopilot will be shared with two teams so you will need to design your plane to allow easy access to attach/detach the autopilot. You’ll want easy access for your batteries anyway. Note: the computers allow multiple profiles so you are just sharing hardware, not software instructions.
    • We will provide you with a receiver that pairs with our transmitters.
    • We have assembled a kit of electronics that you will purchase. Go the ECE Shop and tell them you want to purchase an ME 415 Airplane Kit. Bring the receipt to the TAs and they will give you your kit. The cost per team is $35. This kit includes 2 motors, 4 propellers (some spares), 2 electronic speed controllers, 2 batteries (one spare for swapping in field), a pack of O-rings for mounting the props to the motors, and a pack of bullet connectors.
    • While at the ECE Shop purchase some of the 9 gram servos. These are not included in the kit because the ECE Shop already carries them. You’ll probably want 3, depending on what control surfaces you intend to use.
  2. Characterize your motor performance. Measure the no load current and resistance and compare with values supplied
