Mentoring

At BYU you have a unique opportunity to mentor undergraduates in your research. We involve undergraduates in our research at BYU to a relatively high degree. This is a great preparatory experience for them, but is also an excellent learning experience for you. As future engineering leaders you will need strong skills in leading teams and mentoring. This is particularly true for the Ph.D. students. Ideally, projects will be organized by teams, each team lead by a Ph.D. student

For experimental work it is usually easy to find ways undergraduates can help with data collection. For computational work, which is our main focus, computers can take care of a lot of automation and so finding suitable tasks takes a bit more creativity. Here is a rule of thumb: if you have any task that an undergraduate could do, and if that task would take more than a few minutes, you probably should not being doing it. Delegating these tasks will gives undergraduates good experience, and will free your time for higher-level work. Some examples might include:

  • preparing a publication-ready figure from some data
  • reading and summarizing papers to form a portion of a literature review
  • translating code from one language to another
  • writing some unit tests for your code
  • collecting data or test cases from online sources or other researchers

I will expect you (graduate students) to let me know when you need more undergraduate students to assist in your work. You will have input on which undergraduates we hire, and your input will be helpful on finding future graduate students.
