Ask class to identify which is which. Draw chord flat with freestream at angle of attack so lift is tilted forward. 90% of the class mistakes vertical for lift.

Example

9.5: The figure below notes the average pressure and shear stress acting on the surface of the 1-m-square flat plate. Determine the lift and drag generated. How much does the shear stress contribute to each?

\begin{align*} L &= A(-p_1 \cos\alpha + p_2 \cos\alpha - \tau_1 \sin\alpha - \tau_2 \sin\alpha)\\ &= A((p_2 - p_1) \cos\alpha - (\tau_1 + \tau_2)\sin\alpha\\ &= 3.47 kN - 0.0163 kN\\ &= 3.45 kN\\ \\ D &= A((p_2 - p_1)\sin\alpha + (\tau_1 + \tau_2)\cos\alpha\\ &= 0.427 kN + 0.133 kN\\ &= 0.560 kN\\ \end{align*}

Important points: - shear stress contiributes very little to drag, but significantly to drag - understanding differences between pressure drag and shear stress drag is very important for design - L/D is generally around 20 for an aircraft.

Important Definitions

Dynamic Pressure: \[q_\infty = \frac{1}{2}\rho V_\infty^2\]

Pressure coefficient: \[C_P = \frac{P - P_\infty}{q_\infty}\]

for incompressible flow: \[C_P = 1 - \left(\frac{V}{V_\infty}\right)^2\]

Derive \(C_P\) for incompressible. Note that all we need to know is the velocity field, then we automatically know pressure.

Lift coefficient: \[C_L = \frac{L}{q_\infty S}\]

Drag coefficient: \[C_D = \frac{D}{q_\infty S}\]

Moment coefficient: \[C_M = \frac{M}{q_\infty S l}\]

Positive moment is generally pitch up.

Center of pressure: Location where the distributed loads effective act on the body (e.g., zero moments about the center of pressure).

Aerodynamic Center: Point at which pitching moment is independent of angle of attack (e.g., \(d c_m / d\alpha = 0\))

What about around an arbitrarily airfoil?

  • For the airfoil the flow is not symmetric...but it is still zero.
